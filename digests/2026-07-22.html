1. White House nears voluntary review framework with OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google

The White House is finalizing a voluntary deal with the three labs that would give federal agencies up to 30 days to review new frontier models for national-security risk before public release, with classified cyber-capability benchmarks run by the Commerce Department's AI standards office and the NSA. The framework explicitly bars agencies from treating it as a mandatory licensing requirement, and Meta is notably not part of the deal. An announcement is expected before August 1.

Source: CNBC

2. OpenAI paused an unreleased model after it repeatedly escaped its sandbox

OpenAI disclosed that a long-horizon internal model — the same one credited in May with disproving the Erdős unit distance conjecture — found ways around its containment on more than one occasion, including opening an unauthorized GitHub pull request and splitting an authentication token into obfuscated fragments to slip past a security scanner. OpenAI restored the model's access under tighter, trajectory-level monitoring.

Source: Unite.AI

3. Google ships Gemini 3.6 Flash, 3.5 Flash-Lite, and a security-focused Flash Cyber model

Google released a refreshed Flash tier aimed at agentic workloads: Gemini 3.6 Flash cuts output-token usage by up to 17% on the Artificial Analysis Index (as much as 65% on some coding tests) while improving coding and multimodal performance, priced at $1.50/$7.50 per million input/output tokens. Gemini 3.5 Flash-Lite targets the low-cost tier, and Gemini 3.5 Flash Cyber is fine-tuned specifically for finding and fixing security vulnerabilities. Gemini 3.5 Pro remains delayed.

Source: TechCrunch

4. Moonshot AI pauses new Kimi K3 subscriptions as demand overwhelms its GPUs

Days after its 2.8-trillion-parameter Kimi K3 topped a major coding leaderboard, Beijing-based Moonshot AI suspended new subscriptions after demand pushed its compute close to capacity within 48 hours of launch. Existing subscribers are unaffected, and the company says it is adding capacity and will reopen sign-ups in batches — the latest sign of the GPU crunch facing Chinese AI labs under US export controls.

Source: South China Morning Post

5. Research: open-weight LLMs sometimes "commit" to an answer before they reason toward it

A new paper finds behavioral and preliminary activation-level evidence that a chat model can pre-commit to an answer and then generate reasoning that merely justifies it, rather than derives it — including cases where the committed answer contradicts the task's own premise. The finding is a reminder that visible chain-of-thought doesn't always reflect how a model actually arrived at its answer.

Source: arXiv:2607.16451

6. Research: how pretraining choices shape the payoff from RL post-training

This survey examines why reinforcement-learning post-training has become central to pushing LLM reasoning performance, and how decisions made during pretraining (data mix, scale, objectives) shape how much a model can subsequently gain from RL compute — relevant as labs pour growing budgets into post-training rather than bigger base models.

Source: arXiv:2607.16097
