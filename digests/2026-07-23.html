Treasury threatens sanctions after White House accuses Moonshot AI of distilling Anthropic's Fable

White House OSTP Director Michael Kratsios said Moonshot AI covertly distilled Anthropic's Fable model to build its Kimi K3 system, alleging an internal platform built to distill across US models while switching access methods to avoid detection, plus unauthorized use of Nvidia GB300 chips routed through Thailand. The Treasury Department is now threatening sanctions over the claims.

OpenAI launches Presence, an enterprise platform for voice and chat AI agents

Presence pairs model reasoning with policy controls, operational guardrails, and escalation protocols so companies can deploy agents for customer support, sales, procurement, IT, and HR. It's already running on OpenAI's own phone support line, resolving 75% of inbound calls without a human, and launches via a limited GA program with early customers including BBVA, SoftBank, and IAG.

OpenAI plans $30B data center campus in Georgia

OpenAI is committing at least $20 billion up front - with total project costs likely exceeding $30 billion - to build "Project Camellia," a 3.2-gigawatt data center campus in Effingham County, Georgia, near Savannah. It's the first data center OpenAI is designing and building itself, with power delivered in phases between 2028 and 2032.

Anthropic and OpenAI break Q2 lobbying spending records

Anthropic spent $1.97 million on Washington lobbying in Q2 2026 (up 26% from Q1), outspending Nvidia and nearly matching Oracle, while OpenAI spent $1.2 million (up 18%). Combined AI-lab lobbying hit $3.17 million for the quarter as the companies push on cybersecurity, copyright, and defense procurement policy.

Jack Dorsey's Block launches Buzz, a Slack/GitHub rival built for humans and AI agents

Buzz is a free, open-source group chat platform running on Nostr that gives AI agents their own cryptographic identities alongside human teammates, merging messaging, project management, and coding into one workspace. It's model-agnostic - teams can bring agents powered by Claude Code, Codex, or Block's own goose framework - and was itself built largely by agents.

Reasoning Fine-Tuning Induces Persistent Latent Policy States

This paper models chain-of-thought reasoning as a switching dynamical system, using time-aware contrastive representation learning combined with discrete regime discovery to recover latent "policies" from a model's activation trajectories. The authors compare reasoning-tuned models against their base counterparts to show that reasoning fine-tuning leaves persistent, identifiable internal states rather than just surface-level behavior changes.

Efficient Chain-of-Modality Reasoning via Progressive Compression for Spoken Language Models

Spoken language models still lag text-based LLMs at multi-step reasoning, particularly on spoken math QA. This paper proposes a progressive compression technique for chain-of-modality reasoning that lets spoken LMs carry reasoning across audio and text representations more efficiently.
