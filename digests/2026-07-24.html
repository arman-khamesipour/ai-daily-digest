DeepSeek graduates V4 from preview to a stable, official release

DeepSeek's V4-Pro (1.6T parameters, 49B active) and V4-Flash (284B parameters, 13B active) move out of preview with a 1M-token context window, tool calling, and thinking/non-thinking modes. The stable release introduces peak-hour API pricing that roughly doubles cost during Beijing business hours, while still undercutting Western frontier models by more than 25x per output token.

AMD lays out its full MI400 lineup and "Helios" rack platform at Advancing AI 2026

At its San Francisco event, AMD detailed 6th-gen EPYC "Venice" server CPUs (up to 256 cores) alongside the Instinct MI400X/MI455X accelerator family, the first GPUs on TSMC's 2nm-class process. The Helios rack pairs 72 MI455X GPUs with Venice CPUs for a combined 31TB of HBM4 memory and up to 2.9 FP4 exaFLOPS, positioning AMD as a rack-scale challenger to Nvidia.

Google's Gemini delay widens its AI gap with OpenAI and Anthropic

Gemini 3.5 Pro's months-long slip over coding shortfalls is compounding internal turmoil at DeepMind, with researchers including Gemini co-lead Noam Shazeer departing for OpenAI and Nobel laureate John Jumper leaving for Anthropic. Employees cite both competitive anxiety and fallout from Google's Pentagon deal as driving the exodus.

Beijing's Ministry of Commerce is reportedly consulting Alibaba, ByteDance, and other domestic AI leaders on restricting overseas transfer of model weights, training data, and use of foreign chipmaking services - a mirror image of US export controls aimed at keeping China's advanced AI assets at home.

Notes to Self: Can LLMs Benefit from Experiential Abstractions?

The authors extract natural-language "lessons learned" from an LLM's own solution traces on math problems into a retrievable library, then test both inference-time retrieval and reinforcement learning with abstraction-augmented prompts. Self-extracted abstractions match teacher-extracted ones and improve performance on reasoning benchmarks, transferring across datasets and models.

Read the abstract on arXiv

OpenSkillRisk: Benchmarking Agent Safety When Using Real-World Risky Third-Party Skills

This paper builds a benchmark of 263 risky skills pulled from public agent-skill marketplaces, spanning seven threat categories, each paired with a sandboxed task to see whether agents recognize and avoid the danger. It finds current agent systems struggle to consistently flag unsafe third-party skills before executing them.
